# Foreword (continued)

[page VIII]

My two helpers Zachariae and Baer I recall with particular gratitude. From the former come many new attestations and cross-references, while the latter has contributed over 100 new vocables, or meanings, from texts of Kaśm(iri) Śiv(aism). Their finds are marked with [Z.] resp. [B.].

With appreciation I also recall the typesetter, who dealt with my manuscript unerringly. This was not entirely easy, because a large part of it was jotted down at a time when the paper shortage had risen to its highest, or when what one bought as writing paper would befriend itself with neither pen nor ink. I therefore found myself obliged to use slips of paper that had already been used two or even three times for other works and that in the end were in part so confusingly motley in appearance that only a practiced eye was able to discern the right path.

Finally, one more remark, the omission of which would be against my innermost conviction: I had imagined a new edition of the pw quite differently! It would have been high time to supplement Böhtlingk's work with the help of all the new material that has appeared since 1889. How rich the yield would have been I have seen especially in the *bhāṇa's* that I read, several of which, despite their slight extent, have yielded several hundred nova. But of course, if thorough work is to be accomplished, all the Indologists of the world would have to come together! That this might soon happen is my sincere wish.

Münster (Westphalia), 26 September 1924.

Richard Schmidt.
