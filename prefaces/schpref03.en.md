# Foreword (continued)

[page VI]

I must not leave unmentioned the fact that the edition contains a whole number of misprints, which may here and there need to be taken into account in judging my list. One should not, however, hold this too severely against the deserving editors, for they rightly recall the saying which, by the way, I should like to have applied to my own achievement as well:

> *gacchataḥ skhalanaṃ kvāpi bhavaty eva pramādataḥ;*
> *hasanti durjanās tatra, samādadhati sajjanāḥ.*

Now I had originally intended to publish my anthology from the Yaśastilakam on its own; on 7 March 1921 I wrote on the title page of the press-ready manuscript, as still stands, the sigh of relief: Augiae stabulis purgatis plaudite, amici — when my plan took an entirely new direction through the Helioplan reprint of the pw. The publisher, namely, approached me with the request that I supply a supplement to it and, above all, issue an appeal in order to win the support of fellow specialists. It appeared in the LZ, in the issue of 1 August 1923, but unfortunately remained as good as unnoticed, only Geldner, Jolly, Zachariae, and cand. E. Baer (Zürich) coming forward. Strange, most strange! But at least as peculiar was it that, as the firm Markert & Petters informed me, various experts [? ?] in Leipzig were of the opinion that a supplement to the pw, transcribed, would have no success!!! Since I really have no understanding for such wisdom, I broke off the negotiations at once and concluded my contract with the Orient-Buchhandlung, whose proprietor, Herr Heinz Lafaire, has from the outset shown so much accommodation and understanding that I consider it my duty to acknowledge this publicly with deepest gratitude. He also had at once the same thought as I, namely that Böhtlingk's supplements would have to be worked into my list without remainder if our publication was to become something really useful. The time available, however, unfortunately did not nearly suffice to check Böhtlingk's citations or even, what would have been far more desirable, to adapt them to the most recent editions: I simply took everything over and only now and then made small external improvements. For the rest, it will be an easy matter to establish at once in each individual case the right of ownership; where * or ° should not suffice, the citation or its form decides definitively. Moreover, I should not be at all annoyed if articles that come from me were ascribed to Böhtlingk. I have an extraordinarily high regard for the achievement embodied in the pw, and a pretty good share still remains for me. Without violating modesty, I may well state that to the roughly 14450 articles in Böhtlingk's supplements I have about 12000 new, or original, ones to set against them. These derive from the following texts:

Amaru(śatakam) ed. Simon und ed. Kāvyamālā.

Amit(agati), Subhāṣitasaṃdoha ed. Hertel u. Schmidt, ZDMG 59. 61.

Unmattar(āghavam) ed. Kāvyamālā Nr. 17.

E(rotik), Beiträge zur indischen. 1st edition. The letters standing in brackets after it mean: A = Anaṅgaraṅga; D = Dinālāpanikā-Śukasaptati; K = Kandarpacūḍāmaṇi; P = Pañcasāyaka; R = Ratirahasya; Rm = Ratimañjarī; S = Smaradīpikā. Some of the more recent erotic authors have meanwhile appeared in Benares and Lahore; they are cited occasionally.

Kathāk(autukam) my edition, Kiel 1898.
